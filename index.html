Welcome! My name is Vikas PD Gawai. I am a Ph.D. candidate of Ag. and Applied Economics Dept. at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

I am an Applied Economist doing policy evaluation using econometric tools. My research broadly focuses on health, aging, human capital, and social inequity in the US and India. A major part of my work explores the impact of technology on health and human capital throughout the life cycle.

My academic path took me through several disciplines. First, I did my undergrad in Civil Engineering from VJTI, Mumbai University, after which I worked in different managerial positions in the construction industry in India. Everyday interaction with the daily wage workers and understanding of their real-life problems, in addition to my own social and personal life experiences, motivated and provided me with critical lenses to pursue a career in Social Sciences. Later, I received a competitive fully-funded scholarship to pursue a Master's in Economics from the Department of Economics, where I received one of the `best paper scholarships' for my master's paper. This recognition further motivated me to pursue a Ph.D.
