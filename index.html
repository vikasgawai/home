Welcome! My name is Vikas PD Gawai. I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Department of Ag. and Applied Economics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison and will be on the job market for 2023-24.

I am an Applied Economist. My research broadly focuses on policy evaluation related to health, aging, human capital, social security, and social inequity in the U.S. and India. A major part of my work explores the impact of technology on health, human capital, and social security insurance.

My academic path took me through several disciplines. First, I did my undergrad in Civil Engineering from VJTI, Mumbai University, after which I worked in different managerial positions in the construction industry in India. Everyday interaction with the daily wage workers and understanding of their real-life problems, in addition to my own social and personal life experiences, motivated and provided me with critical lenses to pursue a career in Social Sciences. Later, I received a competitive fully-funded scholarship to pursue a Master's in Economics at the Department of Economics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where I received one of the `best paper scholarships' for my master's paper. This recognition further motivated me to pursue a Ph.D.

I received several awards during my Ph.D. (refer to my CV for a complete list). I was selected as one of the ten scholars from institutions across the U.S. for the JSIT (2022), under the Center for Financial Security (CFS), at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Further, I won one of the five Center for Financial Security Retirement and Disability Research Center (CFS RDRC) research awards to conduct research related to the Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI), working with restricted data from Social Security Administration (SSA). I was also selected as one of the ten Diversity Scholars from the institutions across the U.S. to attend the Diversity Academy (2023) at the School of Public Affairs, at the American University.
